1.5 Copyright and other constraints affecting websites

"Copyright is one of the main types of intellectual property. It allows the copyright owner to protect against others copying or reproducing their work."[7] "Copyright is a legal right, giving the owner control over their work and how it is used. It gives creators protection against use of their work without permission. Owners of copyright can use, sell or licence a work to a third party. Copyright normally protects the work created by the author. 1.6 Issues that people with disabilities may face when using webpages and how we can overcome them

When developing a web page it is important to take into consideration different impairments users may have in order to make it more user friendly for them such as people who are visually impaired. Here are a number of things you can do to make your website more user friendly.
Include proper alt text for images- Alt text should be provided for images, so that screen reader users can understand the message conveyed by the use of images on the page.
Use headings correctly to organise the structure of your content- By using headings correctly and strategically, the content of your website will be well-organised and easily interpreted by screen readers.
Use colour with care- Be aware that the red-green colour deficiency, affects approximately 8% of the population. Using only these colours will prevent these individuals from understanding your message. Other groups of people with disabilities can benefit greatly from colour when it is used to distinguish and organise your content.
To satisfy both groups, use colour, but also be sure to use other visual indicators, for example asterisks or question marks. You can also distinguish blocks of content from one another using visual separation like whitespace or borders.[12]
